Joy Taylor: Professional Career

Born in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, Joy Taylor grew up in a sports-loving family. She developed an interest in broadcasting at an early age. She attended Barry University in Miami, Florida, where she graduated with a Bachelor of Arts in broadcast communications in 2009. While in college, she hosted a radio show called The Noise and managed the campus radio station WBRY 1640 AM. She also interned at several local radio and TV stations, gaining valuable experience and exposure in the media industry.

She began her career as the executive producer for 790 AM The Ticket in Miami, where she worked for three years. She then became the co-host of the morning-drive sports radio show, “Zaslow and Joy Show.” She also hosted “Fantasy Football Today” and “Thursday Night Live” on CBSSports.com.

Taylor joined FOX Sports in March 2016, playing pinch-hitter in several different FS1 roles, including filling in for Kristine Leahy on “The Herd with Colin Cowherd.” In August 2016, she became the moderator for “Skip and Shannon: Undisputed” with commentators Skip Bayless and Shannon Sharpe.

In 2018, Taylor moved from “Undisputed” to “The Herd with Colin Cowherd,” where she served as the news update anchor and co-host. She also launched her own podcast, “Maybe I’m Crazy,” and hosted “The Hang” on Facebook Live. In 2021, Taylor started her own Saturday program, “The Joy Taylor Show” on Fox Sports Radio.

Joy Taylor is currently based in Los Angeles, California. She is the co-host of Speak with LeSean McCoy and Emmanuel Acho on Fox Sports 1, which is headquartered in Los Angeles. She also attends various events and concerts in the city, such as the Beyonce concert at SoFi Stadium and the Blue Beetle special screening at TCL Chinese Theatre. She also enjoys living it up in Las Vegas, which is not far from Los Angeles.

Joy Taylor: A Voice of Diversity

One of Joy Taylor’s personal goals is to foster diversity in the industry of communication. She recognizes the importance of having diverse voices and perspectives in the media and the challenges that underrepresented groups face in the field. She strives to be a role model and a mentor for aspiring communicators of color and to create more opportunities for them to showcase their talents and skills.

Taylor has shared her advice for aspiring sportscasters, especially women and people of color, who face discrimination and harassment in the industry. She has spoken about the importance of representation and empowerment for young black women in sports media. In an interview, Taylor shared her own journey and inspiration as a sportscaster.

Taylor said that she was influenced by Jemele Hill, the former ESPN host and columnist who is now a writer and podcast host for The Atlantic. Taylor said that watching Hill on ESPN gave her the confidence and motivation to pursue her dream of becoming a sports opinionist.

“My hope is the more that young black women see us on television that idea that you can do this also,” Taylor said. “I mean that’s what it was for me watching Jemele Hill on ESPN like I was able to see like okay not only is she on television every day she’s doing what I want to do which is give her opinion and she’s equal to all the men on the show and for me that made me to believe it could happen right.”

Taylor also acknowledged the challenges and barriers that women and people of color face in the sports industry, which is still dominated by white men. She said that she has learned to overcome the doubts and criticisms from others and herself, and to trust her abilities and voice.

“It’s so much harder to create something and know you can do something and no one else can see you in that space,” Taylor said. “But when someone’s already done it before you and you see the representation of yourself on television that inspires the ability to believe that you can do it and to follow that path.”

Joy Taylor: A Foundation for Change

Joy Taylor is not only a successful media personality, but also a generous philanthropist and a passionate advocate for diversity and social justice.

As a Barry University alumna, Joy Taylor has shown her gratitude and support for her alma mater by establishing a scholarship for students pursuing a bachelor’s degree in Communication disciplines. The scholarship aims to help the next generation of journalists and broadcasters achieve their dreams and aspirations. Joy Taylor also received an Honorary Doctorate from Barry University and delivered the commencement speech for the Spring 2022 graduating class.

In 2021, Joy Taylor founded the Joy Taylor Foundation, a nonprofit organization that serves the homeless, supports survivors of domestic violence, and promotes education and youth empowerment in the communities of Los Angeles, Miami, Pittsburgh, and NYC. The foundation’s mission is to make a positive impact on the lives of those in need and to inspire others to do the same. Joy Taylor believes that everyone deserves a chance to succeed and to live with dignity and respect.
